Morocco

Many say that the richest aspect of Morocco is culture is their music, and it is easy to understand why. Made up of more than twenty-five different types ,Moroccan music reflects the deiversity of the country, whilst echoing their past and present history. The overall sound of the music varies drastically dependent on the region, some of the most well known music comes from Andalusia, Berber, Rai, Chaabi, and Gnawa. Traditional Moroccan music contains a wide variety of different instruments used to produce the creative, inspiring songs.

Andalusian Music

Despite the fact Andalusian music does not originate in Morocco, it is extremely popular there, particularly during religious cerimonies. Andalusian music is often sung in classical Arabic, and when done so it is considered traditional Moroccan music. A blend of Arab and Spanish music, Andulsian music dates back to the 9th century when the Famous Persian singer Ziriab popularised this genre. It makes use of a wide variety of instruments including the violin and flute.

Berber Music

There are three different Berber regions in Morocco, and each of these possess their own language, and concequently, their own Berber rhythms. Thanks to a few Berber musicicans and poets Berber music has survived, some of these musicians include Ferhat Mehenni and Idir. However, most Arab Moroccans do not listen to Berber music, simply because they cannot speak or understand Berber. Just like Andulsian music, Berber music uses a wide variety of different intruments; the main intruments used in Berber music are the Bendir (a round drum), and the banjo.
