South Africa

The South African music scene features two main themes, popular and folk, and the distinction between these is clear. Throughout the years, the political environment of the country had majorly influenced South Africa's music, leading to the birth of new, original genres, such as kwaito, African jazz, and mbube.

Popular Music

During the 1980s a new genre emerged with South Africa, Bubblegum. This was the label for the politically active, electronically advanced music produced during this time, the label implyed the music was disposable and mass produced. Understandably, most creators despised this term, preferring the label "disco". The hit song featured in the video, Weekend Special, by Brenda Fassie signaled the start of the Bubblegum era.


Traditional Music

Despite what the name implies, traditional music is not any less evolved or popular than popular music. Throughout modern history, marketing agencies and those wanting to promote tourism have pushed traditional South African music to the forefront of world music. Just like modern music genres, traditonal music from South Africa is constantly evolving. The orginial inhabitants of South Africa, known as Khosion created and inspired many traditional African music pieces. Music played a central role in their rituals, where they would transence into trance style dance moves. These dance moves mimic movements of some native South African animals, such as antelope.
