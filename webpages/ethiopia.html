Ethiopia

Music within Ethiopia is extremly diverse, however each music genre share many common features and use similar intruments. These intruments are unique to ethiopian music, read on to learn more about these and how they are used to create magnificant sounds of their music Some of the most significant instruments in Ethiopian music are the masenqo, the azmari, the krar, and the washnit.

The Masenqo

This is a single-stringed lute and is played with a bow. The masenqo was originally developed in the northern highlands of Ethiopia but has more recently popularity throughout the rest of Ethiopia and Eritrea. Even though the intrument looks simple as it only has one one string, it requries alot of skill to mastered, however once mastered it can produce a variety of complex melodies.


The Krar

Another instrument commonly used in Ethiopian music is the krar, this is often used in traditional love songs as it produces a soft sound. This intrument has five or six strings that are plucked or strummed. The video shows a traditional Ethiopian song featuring the krar.
