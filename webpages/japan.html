Japan

Japan has two distinct world music styles, traditional and modern. Japan is home to the world's largest physical music market. Local Japanese music often appears at karaoke venues, which are very popular Overall, Japanese music is very different from Western music, as it is often based on the intervals of human breathing instead of mathematical timing.

Tradtional Music

Many Japanese music forms were imported from China over a thousand years ago, however over the years these rhythms have been reshaped into Japanese styles of expression. Intruments were adapted and created to meet their needs, the most important of these intruments being the shamisen, shakuhaci, and koto.

The Shamisen

With a long, thin neck and a small rectangular body, the shamisen resembles a guitar. It has three strings, and to adjust the pitch, there are tuning pegs of the head, just like on a guitar or violin. The strings on a shamisen are not plucked with fingers, instead a large triangular plectrum is used to pluck the strings. The shasimen is frequently used to accompany various types of songs.

The Shakuhachi

This instrument is a flute that is made of bamboo, it is played by blowing on one end, on the other end there are four holes on the front, and one on the back, because of this many English people refer to the Shakuhachi as the 'five-holed bamboo flute'. These five holes produce a complete range of sounds; it is actually because these holes are so small that the Shakuhachi manages to produce its distinctively poignant tone.
