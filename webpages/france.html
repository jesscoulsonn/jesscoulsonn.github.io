France

France is a country filled with people who immigrated from many different countries, this has made France a truely multicultural nation. This diverse population has allowed for a partiulary creative music scene. In the classical music field France has many well enstablished romatic composers, whilst folk and pop music have become more popular, and have lead to the rise of the chanson and caberet style. Their are many French music artists that are internationally renowned, particulalry those producing and performing nouvelle, chanson and electronic music. Similarly to most other countries, the music produced within France has evolved with time, yet many musical pieces form centuries ago are still extremely popular today, with modern artists still perfoming songs written hundreds of years ago.


Classical Music

The evolution of French classical music peaked during the Romantic Era in the 19th century. One of the most popular French composers of this time, and arguably the most innovative composer of the early Romantic era was Hector Berlioz. For many, Berlioz holds a unique place in musical history; he was far ahead of his time.

After watching a performance of Romeo and Juliet at the Odeon Theatre in Paris Berloiz was inspired to create a musical piece called Romeo et Juliette. This is a symmphonie dramtique, a large-scale choral symphony, and it is often argued as Berloiz's most comprehensive and detailed piece.The video shows the London Symphony Orchestra covering Romeo et Juliette, this shows how the music produced by Hector Berloiz is still admired today.

Folk Music

Traditional styles of folk music have survived well in most remote areas within France, such as the island of Corsica, and the moutainous region of Auvergne. Many areas have revived folk tradtions recently to cater to tourists, pulling in attraction to their area. These revived folk tradtions often have a heavy focus on very early 20th century melodies, paired with the the use of the piano accordion. A piano accordion is an accordion that is equipped with a right-hand keyboard similar to that found on a piano or organ. It is believed that, the first piano accordion was introduced -in France in 1852, by Bouton of Paris.
