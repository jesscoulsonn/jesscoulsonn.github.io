Cuba

Cuban music is often considered one of the richest and most influential music regions in the world. Since the 19th century Cuban music has been hugely influential throughout the world, contributing the development of a large variety of different genres and music styles throughout the world. Orginating in the 19th century as a combination of Spanish verse and chorus, and African vocals and drumming, the Cuban music genre known as Son inspired all other music genres in Cuba.

Salsa

Salsa music is loved by millions globally, its rythm, dance and generally excitement cannot be resisted. Just like many other Cuban music genres, salsa music borrows many ideas and themes from the son genre. Salsa music often mimics patterns found in tradtional African songs, through the heavy use of percussion instruments such as the clave, maracas, conga, bongo, and the cowbell.

Common features of salsa music include:

  • A time signature of 4/4
  • The use of clave rhythms, which provide the rhythmic foundation
  • The use of call and response vocals
  • The use of love lyrics
