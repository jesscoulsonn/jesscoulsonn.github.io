UK

The types of music produced in the UK varies depending on the region, the most noteable regions that have produced world music within the UK is Ireland and Scotland. Here, celtic folk music has been produced, and some songs have gained international recagnition. One of these artists includes Capercaillie, they are a Scottish band who combine tradtional Gaelic folk music and rock music. Concequently, there music is a fusion of the two styles and is often described as celtic rock.

Capercaillie

There music combines elements from both folk and rock music, and the instruments they use reflect this. Intruments frequently used by Capercaillie include the piano, bass, accordion, and a drum kit. This video shows the Skyewalking song, this is probably the most popular song by Capercaillie. This song is from a collection of Gaelic folk songs complied by the folkorist Alexander Carmicheal, and has eight verses and features an intrumental section.



Manran

Manran are a Scottish band that were only enstablished in 2010, however since then they have performed at several folk and world music festivals. They have provided great visability for world music. This video shows one of Manran's most popular songs, Latha Math.
